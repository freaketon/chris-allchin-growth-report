🧠
Curiosity Gap Hooks

Opens an information gap the viewer's brain physically cannot ignore. The viewer needs to keep watching to close the loop. Works best when you combine specificity with a hidden result.

1

"There's a $5 trillion opportunity that nobody on Instagram is talking about. Here's how you can get a piece of it before it disappears…"

Why it works: Combines a massive number ($5T) with exclusivity ("nobody is talking about") and urgency ("before it disappears"). The viewer must keep watching to learn what the opportunity is.
2

"10,000 baby boomers retire every single day. Most of them own a business. And most of those businesses will never sell. Here's why that's your opportunity…"

Why it works: Opens with a shocking statistic, then creates a curiosity gap by framing a problem as an opportunity. The audience must watch to understand the connection.
3

"I found a business doing $2M a year in revenue listed for sale on BizBuySell. Nobody had made an offer in 6 months. Here's what I found when I dug deeper…"

Why it works: Specific numbers create credibility. The mystery of "why no offers" creates an irresistible curiosity gap. The audience needs to know what he found.
4

"The reason most people never buy a business has nothing to do with money. It has everything to do with this one thing…"

Why it works: Challenges the most common assumption (money) and promises a surprising alternative. The viewer cannot scroll past without knowing what "this one thing" is.
5

"A 68-year-old business owner called me last week. He'd been trying to sell his $800K/year business for 2 years. Not a single serious buyer. Here's what I told him…"

Why it works: Specific details (age, revenue, timeframe) make it feel real. The unresolved situation creates a powerful curiosity gap. The audience needs to hear what advice was given.
😤
Problem Amplification Hooks

Names a frustration your audience already feels but hasn't articulated. When someone hears their own struggle described precisely, they stop scrolling because the video feels made specifically for them.

1

"You're working 60-hour weeks building someone else's dream. You've thought about starting a business but the risk terrifies you. Nobody told you there's a third option…"

Why it works: Speaks directly to the exact frustration of the ICP (corporate professional wanting out). "Third option" creates a curiosity gap that keeps them watching.
2

"You've been saving money for years to start a business. But every time you look at the startup failure rate, you talk yourself out of it. Here's what you're missing…"

Why it works: Describes the exact internal dialogue of the aspiring entrepreneur. The "here's what you're missing" creates urgency and positions Chris as the guide.
3

"If you're trying to build wealth by starting a business from scratch in 2026, you're playing the hardest version of the game. Here's the easier path…"

Why it works: Validates the audience's struggle while positioning the acquisition path as the smarter alternative. Loss aversion (playing the "hardest version") is a powerful motivator.
4

"Most people who want to buy a business never actually do it. Not because they can't afford it. Because nobody taught them how to find the deal…"

Why it works: Reframes the obstacle from money (which feels permanent) to knowledge (which feels solvable). This positions Chris as the solution and makes the audience feel empowered.
5

"The business broker is not on your side. The seller's accountant is not on your side. And most acquisition advisors charge $50K upfront. Here's how I navigate this alone…"

Why it works: Creates a "us vs. them" dynamic that the audience immediately relates to. Positions Chris as the insider guide who has cracked the code.
⚡
Authority Challenge Hooks

Challenges conventional wisdom or calls out an authority figure. Humans are wired to pay attention to conflict and contrarian viewpoints. These hooks activate the brain's threat-evaluation system — impossible to ignore.

1

"Unpopular opinion: starting a business in 2026 is one of the worst financial decisions you can make. Here's what I'd do instead…"

Why it works: Directly challenges the dominant narrative of entrepreneurship culture. The contrarian stance creates immediate engagement — people either agree and want validation, or disagree and want to argue.
2

"I've bought and sold 10+ businesses. And I'm telling you — the 'start from scratch' advice you're getting from most business gurus is going to cost you years of your life…"

Why it works: Leads with hard-won credentials (10+ businesses) and then challenges a widely-held belief. The authority makes the challenge credible rather than just contrarian.
3

"Stop listening to people who've never actually bought a business tell you how to buy a business. Here's what it actually looks like from someone who's done it 10 times…"

Why it works: Calls out a credibility gap in the market and positions Chris as the authentic alternative. His real-world experience is the differentiator.
4

"The MBA program won't teach you this. The business school case study won't cover it. But buying a boring, cash-flowing business is the fastest path to financial freedom in 2026…"

Why it works: Positions the acquisition path as insider knowledge that the establishment deliberately ignores. Creates a sense of exclusivity and makes the viewer feel like they're getting access to something rare.
5

"Every business coach is telling you to 'build your personal brand' and 'create content.' I'm telling you to buy a business that already makes money. Here's why I'm right…"

Why it works: Directly challenges the content creator economy narrative that dominates Instagram. Positions Chris as a contrarian voice with a more practical, results-oriented approach.
🔄
Transformation & Before-After Hooks

Shows the end result first, then creates a compulsion to learn the how. Taps into aspirational desire — the same mechanism that makes makeover shows impossible to stop watching.

1

"2 years ago I was working a corporate job I hated. Today I own 3 cash-flowing businesses. Here's the exact path I took…"

Why it works: Classic before-after that speaks directly to the ICP's dream scenario. The "exact path" promise makes it feel actionable and achievable, not just aspirational.
2

"I almost walked away from the deal that changed my life. Here's the moment I nearly said no — and what made me say yes…"

Why it works: Creates narrative tension (almost walked away) and emotional investment. The audience needs to know what almost stopped him and what changed his mind.
3

"I lost $200,000 on my first business acquisition. Here's the 3 mistakes I made and exactly how I made it back on the next deal…"

Why it works: Vulnerability builds trust. The specific loss amount ($200K) creates credibility. The "made it back" redemption arc is a powerful narrative that keeps viewers watching.
4

"Watch me analyze a real business for sale in under 5 minutes — and tell you exactly whether it's a deal or a disaster…"

Why it works: The "watch me" format creates a live demonstration dynamic. The time constraint (5 minutes) makes it feel accessible. The binary outcome (deal or disaster) creates suspense.
5

"From $0 to owning a business doing $1.2M in revenue — without a business degree, without a trust fund, and without quitting my job first. Here's how…"

Why it works: Removes the three biggest objections (education, money, risk) in the hook itself. The audience immediately identifies with the constraints and is compelled to learn how he overcame them.
👁️
POV & Perspective Shift Hooks

Places the viewer inside a scenario or challenges their worldview. Creates immediate identification and emotional engagement. Particularly powerful for the "aspiring buyer" ICP who is stuck in analysis paralysis.

1

"POV: You just bought a business for $0 down using seller financing. The previous owner is now your mentor. And you're making $15K a month in your first 90 days…"

Why it works: Places the viewer directly in the dream scenario. The specific details ($0 down, mentor relationship, $15K/month) make it feel real and achievable rather than abstract.
2

"You're not an entrepreneur. You're not an investor. You're something better — you're a business buyer. And that changes everything…"

Why it works: Creates a new identity for the viewer that feels exclusive and empowering. "That changes everything" creates a curiosity gap that keeps them watching.
3

"Imagine buying a business that's already been running for 20 years, already has customers, already has employees, and already makes money. That's not a fantasy. That's Tuesday for me…"

Why it works: The "imagine" opener creates a vivid mental picture. The contrast between "fantasy" and "Tuesday for me" positions Chris as the aspirational figure while making the dream feel accessible.
4

"The way most people think about building wealth is completely backwards. They're trying to build something from nothing. I'm buying something that already works…"

Why it works: Reframes the entire concept of wealth-building and positions the acquisition path as the logical, obvious choice. The contrast between "build from nothing" and "buy something that works" is simple and memorable.
5

"In 10 years, you'll either wish you bought one of the 6 million businesses baby boomers are selling right now — or you'll own one. Which person do you want to be?"

Why it works: Creates a future regret scenario (powerful motivator) and forces a binary identity choice. The specific number (6 million) adds credibility and scale to the opportunity.
🔢
Number & List Hooks

Numbers create instant specificity and credibility. The brain processes numbers differently from words — they feel concrete, measurable, and trustworthy. List hooks also set clear expectations, reducing cognitive resistance to watching.

1

"5 questions I ask every business owner before I even consider making an offer — and the answers that make me walk away immediately…"

Why it works: The list structure (5 questions) sets clear expectations. The "walk away immediately" creates suspense — the audience wants to know what red flags to avoid.
2

"3 types of businesses baby boomers are selling right now that nobody wants to buy — but that are printing money for the people who do…"

Why it works: The "nobody wants to buy" creates a contrarian opportunity framing. The "printing money" payoff makes it irresistible. Directly ties into the Silver Tsunami trend.
3

"I've bought 10 businesses. Here are the 3 mistakes I made on my first deal that I'll never make again — and that you should avoid at all costs…"

Why it works: Credentials (10 businesses) + vulnerability (mistakes) + actionable value (avoid these) = a perfect hook formula. The "never make again" signals hard-won wisdom.
4

"7 things that happen in the first 90 days after you buy a business that nobody warns you about — and how to handle each one…"

Why it works: "Nobody warns you about" creates exclusivity and insider knowledge framing. The 90-day timeframe is specific and relatable. The "how to handle each one" promises actionable value.
5

"$0 down. $1.2M in revenue. 14 employees. Here's the exact deal structure I used to buy this business without spending a dollar of my own money…"

Why it works: Opens with three specific numbers that immediately establish credibility and scale. The "exact deal structure" promise makes it feel like a masterclass, not just a story.
